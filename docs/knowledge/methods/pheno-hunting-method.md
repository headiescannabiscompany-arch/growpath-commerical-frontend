# Pheno Hunting

Pheno hunting follows the plant from germination through final product. Track early vigor/morphology, sex timing, stem rub, structure, stretch, feed and stress response, recovery, flower structure, resin, aroma, taste, effect, yield, clone performance, TC suitability and breeding value.

Sex expression near veg week four can be useful relative to waiting until week six. Final product outweighs early hype. Strong smell/taste and resilience matter. A flower keeper may fail as a commercial clone; a breeding parent may not be the best production plant.

Keep distinct decisions: flower, hash, clone, mother, breeding parent, stress-resistant, commercial, greenhouse/outdoor, personal-only, retest and reject. Record evidence and reasoning, not only a composite score.
