# Clone Rooting

## Purpose and access

Use this workflow to review a real cannabis/hemp stem-cutting batch, identify evidence-supported concerns, and schedule a repeatable follow-up. The tool requires an owned cannabis or hemp grow at both the screen and API boundaries. It is not a grow-independent plant-identification or general gardening tool.

The calculator itself uses no AI credit. Optional grow/photo prefill may use one credit only when the configured provider runs; failed provider calls are refunded. Every prefilled value remains a draft that the user must review.

## Required evidence

Start blank. Never silently supply batch counts, elapsed days, environmental measurements, donor health, sanitation status, media moisture, or visible conditions.

Require:

- days since cut as context, not a rooting deadline;
- total cuttings in the reviewed batch;
- directly visibly rooted count;
- failed or culled count; and
- a direct-root evidence state: `no_visible_roots`, `callus_visible`, `roots_visible`, `mixed`, or `not_checked`.

Validate whole-number counts. Rooted plus failed cannot exceed total. Visible callus cannot exceed the remaining live/unrooted count. Wilted count cannot exceed total.

When available, record callus and wilt counts, timestamp and sensor/observation source, RH at cutting height, air and representative plug/root-zone temperature, PPFD, photoperiod, humidity method, medium/system and moisture condition, rooting product/method, mother health, sanitation, stem-base condition, leaf/turgor condition, tray distribution, prior changes, and donor/batch identifiers.

## Root and photo evidence rules

Only directly exposed/visible roots count as rooted. Elapsed time, top growth, leaf posture, callus, and tug resistance do not prove hidden roots or automatic failure. If roots were not inspected, keep them unknown and schedule a direct check rather than guessing.

Photos can document a tray-wide pattern, representative strong and weak cuttings, stem-base appearance, visible callus, leaf condition, and exposed roots. Photos cannot supply batch counts or sensor values, see inside opaque plugs/media, or diagnose a pathogen from appearance. Attaching media alone is not analysis; record provider/model provenance, photos analyzed, visible evidence used, quality/confidence, and limitations only when pixel inspection was actually confirmed.

## Assessment and output

Evaluate batch pattern, mother health, sanitation, medium moisture, measured environment, direct root evidence, and elapsed time together. Return:

- assessment status and confidence;
- validated total, rooted, failed, pending, callus and wilt counts with percentages;
- measured environment with units, timestamp and source;
- evidence-supported bottlenecks with the observation and cautious remediation;
- missing information and explicit limitations;
- source and method IDs; and
- a follow-up plan for recounting, photos, environment verification and final survival/transplant outcome.

Do not label a batch behind solely from age. Do not treat a research range as a universal operating setpoint. _Light, Temperature, and Relative Humidity Influence the Adventitious Rooting of Cannabis Stem Cuttings_ tested two Korean hemp cultivars, found cultivar-dependent responses, and did not test combined environmental-variable effects. Use those ranges only as study context. NC State Extension supports the general cutting-propagation principles that high humidity reduces water loss and that rooting media should be clean, low fertility, well drained and moisture retentive; it does not prove an individual cutting has rooted.

## Saved workflow and learning loop

Save the ToolRun, create dated recount/photo/environment/outcome tasks, and record final survival, transplant readiness, rooting speed and repeatability by mother/genetics and batch. Feed those outcomes into mother, phenotype, genetics and commercial-selection records without turning a single batch into a cultivar guarantee.
